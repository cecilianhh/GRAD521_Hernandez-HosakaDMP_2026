# Data Description
This food science and technology project aims to investigate the optimal hydrophobic coating derived from agricultural residues (e.g., food and agricultural biomass) for use on fiber-based packaging. Agricultural residues contain valuable natural compounds such as phenolics, lipids, and cellulose that can be utilized for various applications, including packaging materials. Fiber-based packaging alone has poor barrier properties, and conventional plastic or chemical coatings used to enhance its structure pose health and environmental risks. Thus, compounds extracted from agricultural residues can be formulated into coatings to improve fiber-based packaging materials. The performance of these coatings for this type of packaging will be quantitatively measured for their barrier properties, mechanical strength, contact angle, morphology, antioxidant activity, and antimicrobial activity, and qualitatively by their appearance over a specified period to ensure that the packaging performs at optimal conditions appropriate for commercial use in the food industry. All variables will be measured and calculated using the protocols previously used in the lab and found in the literature. Datasets are a combination of (1) quantitative measurements calculated and stored on Excel spreadsheets and Minitab worksheets, and (2) iPhone photographs of samples uploaded to Box and the researcher’s laptop as .jpg files in folders labeled with the subject name and date taken.

Collecting data consists of various experimental methods and equipment, which includes:
(a)	Barrier properties: Water vapor absorption will be measured by placing samples into a controlled relative humidity chamber and measuring their weight every hour over the course of a specific time period using a scale. Values will be recorded in a lab notebook and then transferred to an Excel spreadsheet for calculations. This data will be <1 MB.
(b)	Mechanical strength: Tensile strength will be measured using a texture analyzer, and force values will be recorded in a lab notebook before transferring values into an Excel spreadsheet for calculations. This data will be <1 MB.
(c)	Contact angle: A drop of water is dropped onto the sample surface and photographed and measured for its angle. The photographs will be stored as .jpg files onto Box and laptop files, and angle measurements will be stored on an Excel spreadsheet. This data will be <1 MB.
(d)	Morphology: Scanning electron microscopy (SEM) will detect the surface morphology of the coated fiber packaging, and images will be saved as .jpg onto Box and laptop files. This data will be <1 GB due to the large image file sizes.
(e)	Antioxidant activity: Following the method used in the lab, samples will be treated with a DPPH solution and analyzed with a spectrophotometer. Spectrophotometric measurements will be recorded in a lab notebook and then transferred to an Excel spreadsheet for antioxidant activity calculations. This data will be <1 MB.
(f)	Antimicrobial activity: Following the method used in the lab, samples will be placed onto Petri dishes, incubated, and the inhibition of growth around the sample will be measured quantitatively using a caliper. Values will be recorded in a lab notebook and then transferred to an Excel spreadsheet for calculations. Phone images of the plates will be taken and stored onto Box and laptop files. This data will be <1 GB total.
(g)	Appearance: The packaging will be stored at different temperature conditions, and iPhone photographs are taken at certain time points to observe visible changes that occur. Images will be stored onto Box and laptop files. This data will be <1 GB due to the large image file sizes.

Values for each measurement will be reported as the mean and standard deviation. A one-way ANOVA with a post hoc LSD test will be used to analyze the values using Minitab to determine statistically significant differences. All Excel spreadsheets will be uploaded to Box and stored on the laptop.

# Roles and Responsibilities
Throughout the project, the primary researcher will be responsible for multiple data-related roles, including data collection, documentation, analysis, and organization. The primary researcher is also responsible for sharing results with the PI through a shared folder on Box. The PI has authority over how the data should be managed. They will be responsible for implementing the Data Management Plan (DMP) and assisting with data planning, analysis, communication, editing, and publishing. The PI is also responsible for ensuring that data collection is conducted properly so that future researchers can trust and rely on the procedures and analyses used. The postdoctoral scholar in the lab is responsible for instrumentation maintenance and quality control, ensuring that instruments are calibrated and in working order, that procedures and lab supplies are up to date, and that lab members have received proper safety and experimental training, so that the data they collect is collected correctly. If necessary, the postdoctoral scholar will also assist with data collection and analysis. If the primary researcher or another researcher leaves the lab, all files generated during their time in the lab will be transferred to the new researcher responsible for the project and the PI. If the PI leaves the lab, the institution (Oregon State University) will retain ownership of the data, but the PI may take copies with them to their new institution if OSU approves.

Since the research conducted by the primary researcher does not involve human or animal subjects, there is no need to handle such sensitive data. The lab requires researchers to upload their files to their personal Box folder, which is accessible to the PI. The project is anticipated to be funded by the USDA NIFA, so the DMP must include the expected data type, data format, data storage and preservation, data sharing, protection, and public access, and roles and responsibilities, in accordance with their guidelines. 

All data will be stored on the primary researcher’s personal computer or lab notebook and backed up onto a flash drive and Box account. All digital data will be transferred to the PI and backed up using an external hard drive, flash drive, and Box folder when the project is completed, while the lab notebook will remain in a safe storage area in one of the lab spaces with other previous researchers’ lab notebooks. This means there are at least five copies available (Box, external drive, flash drive, computer, lab notebook), ensuring the data is stored in multiple ways and reducing the probability of it becoming lost in the future. Data files will be manually uploaded to Box, the personal computer, and drives. When edits are needed to files uploaded to Box, they can either be replaced manually or edited online, where they will automatically update. The primary researcher is responsible for ensuring that all data files are up to date and labeled appropriately to prevent the use of incorrect files or data loss. To ensure that the data saved on Box will not disappear after the primary researcher graduates and leaves, a recovery email will be added to the account to still retain access when their institutional account is inactivated.
